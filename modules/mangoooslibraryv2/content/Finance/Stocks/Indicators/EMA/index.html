EMA

EMA (Exponential Moving Average) is a weighted moving average meaning the average value will be more dependent on the recent closing prices (exponential). EMA is great for more precision in moving averages, and is also used to calculate MACD

To calculate EMA we will have to calculate the SMA first. We will select a 5 day period from our previously calculated SMA

SMA 5-period = 22.8

Add 5-day closing price together (but not today's price) and devide by 5 for the SMA

The EMA formula:

The EMA formula looks complicated but is actually really easy if you do it step by step.



\[ \text{EMA}_t = (\alpha \cdot P_t) + (\text{EMA}_{t-1} \cdot (1 - \alpha)) \] where:

- \( P_t \) is the closing price (or relevant price) at time \( t \),

- \( \text{EMA}_{t-1} \) is the EMA from the previous period,

- \( \alpha = \frac{2}{N + 1} \) is the smoothing factor (also called the multiplier),

- \( N \) is the number of periods (common values: 9, 12, 20, 26, 50).


Or simpler said:

\[ \text{EMA}_t = \text{(multiplier} + \text{stock price today)} + \text{(SMA $\cdot$ (1 - multiplier)} \]






1. Calculating multiplier

The first step in calculating EMA is to calculate the multiplier to properly weight the moving average.



\[ \alpha = \frac{2}{N + 1} \]

where:

- \( \alpha \) is the EMA multiplier (smoothing factor),

- \( N \) is the number of days/periods in the EMA (e.g., 5 for a 5-period EMA).





To solve this we will do the following:

\[ \alpha = \frac{2}{5 + 1} = 0.333 \]


The smoothing factor can be any amount, but 2 is the best value to choose.




2. Calculating the first part of the EMA

Multiply the multiplier with the today's stock price to get the first half of the equation.


For the first part we need to use the multiplier and do times

\[ \text{(multiplier} \cdot \text{stock price today)} \] \[ \text{(0.333} \cdot \text{26)} = 8.658 \]





3. Calculating the second part of EMA

Use the SMA that we calculated earlier and multiply it with the multiplier - 1.


All we need to do is subract 1 from our multiplier. And do times the SMA value which will get us the answer.

\[ \text{(SMA $\cdot$ (1 - multiplier)} \] \[ \text{(22.8} \cdot \text{0.666) = 15.1848 }\]


We've calculated both sides. To finish the EMA formula we need to put them together by doing:

\[ \text{(8.658) + (15.1848) = 23.8428} \]





Our 5-period EMA = 23.8428
