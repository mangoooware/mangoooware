MACD

MACD is used to see if short time (12 days/period) has been doing better or worse than the long time (26 days/period). These are called 12-period EMA and 26-period EMA. With these you can see if momentum is building (trend strengthening) or fading (possible reversal).

To calculate MACD you need the 12 and 26-period EMA of your chosen stock. With those numbers you can calculate MACD

The MACD formula

The MACD formula exists out of 3 main factors, The MACD line, Signal line and histogram. for MACD, you only need the MACD line. The rest is to calculate the average of MACD over future time.



\[ \text{MACD Line} = \text{EMA}_{12}(P) - \text{EMA}_{26}(P) \] \[ \text{Signal Line} = \text{EMA}_{9}(\text{MACD Line}) \] \[ \text{Histogram} = \text{MACD Line} - \text{Signal Line} \] where:

- \( \text{EMA}_{12}(P) \) is the 12-period EMA of the price \( P \),

- \( \text{EMA}_{26}(P) \) is the 26-period EMA of the price \( P \),

- \( \text{EMA}_{9}(\text{MACD Line}) \) is the 9-period EMA of the MACD Line itself (this is the Signal Line).





1. MACD line

We will use a random EMA value to use as an example.


EMA(12) yesterday = 23.60
EMA(26) yesterday = 22.90

With this we can calculate the MACD line

\[ \text{MACD Line} = \text{EMA}_{12}(P) - \text{EMA}_{26}(P) \] \[ \text{MACD Line} = \text{23.60} - \text{22.90} = \text{0.70} \]


Our MACD line is 0.70

A positive MACD line tells us that short-term EMA is better than long-term EMA indicating a bullish trend. This is basically everything you need to know about MACD, but if you want to do the extra steps you can calculate the average of the MACD values.



2. SMA on MACD

For the signal line we will need 9 values of MACD. Since you're calculating the MACD average, you are not required to do exactly 9 values because you keep adding them per day (thus, you don't seed the MACD like with SMA).


To calculate the signal line we will need MACD values. As example we'll use random MACD value of a 9-period timeframe. Since we're using the SMA formula, we will be adding them together and deviding them by their amount:

$ \text{Signal Line}_9 = \frac{0.25 + 0.32 + 0.38 + 0.42 + 0.45 + 0.47 + 0.48 + 0.48 + 0.48}{9} = 0.4167 $




3. Mutliplier

We calculated SMA on MACD. With that we can now move on to calculate the EMA multiplier.



\[ \alpha = \frac{2}{N + 1} \]

where:

- \( \alpha \) is the EMA multiplier (smoothing factor),

- \( N \) is the number of days/periods in the EMA (e.g., 5 for a 5-period EMA).



Filling out the formula:

\[ \alpha = \frac{2}{9 + 1} = 0.2 \]


Our multiplier is 0.2




4. First part of the signal line equation

With the multiplier we can finish the first part of the equation



The full equation of the signal line is:

$ \text{Signal}_t = \alpha \cdot \text{MACD}_t + (1 - \alpha)\cdot \text{Signal}_{t-1} $



We can break this up in 2 parts:

$ \text{Signal Line}_t = \alpha \cdot (\text{MACD Line})_t $


Or simply said:

$ \text{Signal Line}_t = \text{Multiplier} \cdot (\text{Today's MACD value})_t $

$ \text{Signal Line}_t = \text{0.2} \cdot (\text{0.50})_9 = 0.1 $


The first part of the equation = 0.1




5. Second part of the signal line equation

Now solve the second part of the signal line equation



$ \text{Signal Line}_t = (1 - Multiplier) \cdot (\text{Signal Line})_{t-1} $

$ \text{Signal Line}_t = (1 - 0.2) \cdot (\text{0.1})_{t-1} $

$ \text{Signal Line}_t = (0.8) \cdot (\text{0.1})_{t-1} = 0.08 $


The second part of the equation = 0.08




6. Finalize equation

To solve the equation we must put the numbers together that we calculated previously


The signal line equation:

$ \text{Signal}_t = \alpha \cdot \text{MACD}_t + (1 - \alpha)\cdot \text{Signal}_{t-1} $



Or simpler:

$ \text{Signal Line}_t = \text{Multiplier} \cdot (\text{Today's MACD value})_t + (1 - \text{Multiplier}) \cdot (\text{Signal Line})_{t-1} $



Adding our previous calculated numbers of the equation would be:

$ \text{Signal Line}_t = \text{0.2} \cdot \text{0.50} + \text{0.8} \cdot \text{0.08}_{t-1} $



Our final answer would be:

$ \text{Signal Line}_t = \text{0.1} + \text{0.064}_{t-1} = 0.164 $


Our signal line = 0.164




7. Histogram

Now with the signal line, we can do the histogram by doing the following.



$ \text{Histogram}_t = \text{MACD}_t - \text{Signal Line}_t $

$ \text{Histogram}_t = 0.50 - 0.164 = 0.336 $


The histogram = 0.336




With the histogram we can tell:

Positive histogram → MACD is above its signal line → short-term momentum is stronger than the EMA of MACD → bullish tilt.
Negative histogram → MACD is below its signal line → short-term momentum is weaker than the EMA of MACD → bearish tilt.
