SMA

SMA (Simple Moving Average) calculates the moving average of a stock on your chosen timeframe. This is called a period.

1. SMA Time period

We will choose a 5-period SMA meaning we will need to get the last 5 days + todays stock price:
Do not add todays price into SMA. Yesterday is last.

2. SMA formula

Add the closing prices together and devide them by the number of days. In our case that would be:



\[ \text{SMA} = \frac{A_1 + A_2 + \cdots + A_n}{n} \] \[ A_n = \text{the closing prices of the stock ($A$) per day ($_n$)} \] \[ n = \text{the number of total days/periods} \]


Filling in the formula will get us:

\[ \text{SMA} = \frac{20_5 + 22_4 + 24_3 + 25_2 + 23_5}{5} = 22.8 \]


This means our SMA = 22.8



We now know that our stock moves at an average of 22.8 in the past 5 days.
