On-Balance Volume (OBV)

On-Balance Volume (OBV) is used to measure buying and selling pressure by adding volume on days the price closes higher and subtracting volume on days the price closes lower. It helps confirm trends or spot divergences – if price makes new highs but OBV doesn't, it can signal weakening momentum.

OBV is a cumulative indicator: it keeps a running total of volume, directed by price movement.

The OBV formula

OBV is very simple and builds over time.



\[ \text{OBV}_t = \text{OBV}_{t-1} + \text{Volume Adjustment} \]

where the Volume Adjustment is:

- + Today's Volume      if Close > Previous Close (up day)

- 0                                        if Close = Previous Close (flat day)

- - Today's Volume      if Close < Previous Close (down day)





1. Starting point

The very first OBV value is usually set to 0 or just the first day's volume (depending on the platform). We'll start with OBV = 0 before the first day.


OBV yesterday = 0



2. First day (up day example)

Let's use random daily data to build OBV step by step.


Day 1: Close = 52.10, Volume = 12,000
(No previous close, so we often just add the volume or set OBV to volume. We'll add it.)

\[ \text{OBV}_1 = 0 + 12,000 = 12,000 \]


OBV after day 1 = 12,000





3. Second day (up day)




Day 2: Close = 53.20 (higher than 52.10), Volume = 15,000

Since price closed higher, we add the volume:

\[ \text{OBV}_2 = 12,000 + 15,000 = 27,000 \]


OBV after day 2 = 27,000

Rising OBV confirms buying pressure is building.



4. Third day (down day)




Day 3: Close = 52.80 (lower than 53.20), Volume = 10,000

Since price closed lower, we subtract the volume:

\[ \text{OBV}_3 = 27,000 - 10,000 = 17,000 \]


OBV after day 3 = 17,000

OBV falls, showing selling pressure on this day.



5. Fourth day (flat day)




Day 4: Close = 52.80 (same as day 3), Volume = 8,000

No price change → volume is not added or subtracted:

\[ \text{OBV}_4 = 17,000 + 0 = 17,000 \]


OBV after day 4 = 17,000

Flat days don't affect OBV.



6. Fifth day (up day)




Day 5: Close = 54.00 (higher than 52.80), Volume = 20,000

Price up → add the volume:

\[ \text{OBV}_5 = 17,000 + 20,000 = 37,000 \]


OBV after day 5 = 37,000

Strong buying volume pushes OBV to a new high.


With OBV we can tell:

Rising OBV while price rises → strong bullish trend (volume confirms the move).
Falling OBV while price falls → strong bearish trend.

Rising OBV but flat/falling price → potential bullish divergence (accumulation).
Falling OBV but rising price → potential bearish divergence (distribution).

The absolute OBV number matters less than its direction and whether it confirms price trends.
