Cost Of Revenue (COR) / Cost Of Goods Sold (GOGS)

To calculate both (they are relatively the same) you must identify all expenses directly associated with producing and selling the goods or services. This includes things like:


- Raw materials, wages.
- Equipment; hardware and such, utility (like electricity).
- Market price fluctuations, supplier agreements, and regulatory changes.
- Potential tarrifs, currency exchange rate.


The key difference between COR and GOGS is that their accounting standards are different. COR offers flexibility while COGS is more strict and requires more clarity
